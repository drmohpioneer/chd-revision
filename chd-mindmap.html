CHD   mind map (landscape). Central topic, one branch per lesson, with the exam-critical points. Scroll sideways on mobile.
🟨C1 · Circulation & cyanosis
The framework: sort every lesion by lung blood flow.
  • Umbilical VEIN = highest O2 (Q12)
  • Cyanosis ≥ 3-5 g/dL desaturated Hb
  • Duct-dependent lesions → PGE1
🔵C2 · Cyanotic, ↓ flow
Blue with dark lungs.
  • TOF: boot-shaped heart, RAD, tet spell
  • Tet spell: NOT digoxin (worsens RVOT)
  • Tricuspid / pulmonary atresia
🟦C3 · Cyanotic, ↑ flow
Blue with wet lungs.
  • D-TGA (parallel circuits → switch)
  • TAPVR (snowman), truncus, DORV
  • D-TGA + VSD + PS = decreased flow
💕C4 · Acyanotic shunts
Pink left-to-right shunts, by their murmur.
  • ASD = fixed split S2 (secundum = RAD)
  • VSD = holosystolic at the LLSB
  • PDA = machinery; AVSD = Down
🚧C5 · Obstructive & other
The leftover pile.
  • Coarctation (Turner) / PS (Noonan)
  • Ebstein (lithium, WPW, ASD)
  • Vascular ring (barium+CT, not echo); isomerism
🔀C6 · Single-ventricle pathway
One pump → passive lungs → needs LOW PVR.
  • Stage 1 tailored: Norwood / PA band / BT shunt
  • Glenn (SVC→PA) then Fontan (+IVC, fenestration)
  • Q2 low PVR = B; Q10 PLE = Fontan = E
🧍C7 · Adult CHD
The late lesion the repair left behind.
  • Repaired TOF → pulmonary regurgitation (PVR)
  • D-TGA: Rastelli for TGA+VSD+PS (Q17=B)
  • Eisenmenger = shunt reversal, inoperable
